Frequently Asked Questions

The best time to visit is during the dry seasons (December-February and June-August). These months offer clearer views of the mountains and more comfortable hiking conditions. However, we operate tours throughout the year, and the region's lush greenery is particularly vibrant after the rains.

You can book a tour through our online booking form, by sending us an email at info@lucto.org, or by calling us directly. We recommend booking at least two weeks in advance, especially during peak season, to ensure availability.

We recommend bringing comfortable walking shoes or hiking boots, weather-appropriate clothing (layers are best), a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, a water bottle, and a camera. For overnight stays, bring warm clothing as temperatures can drop significantly at night. A detailed packing list will be provided upon booking.

A portion of all tour fees goes directly to our conservation initiatives, including reforestation, environmental education, and park boundary maintenance. We also work closely with local communities to promote sustainable practices and reduce environmental degradation. Our staff and guides are trained in conservation principles and share this knowledge with visitors.

Yes, we welcome volunteers who are interested in contributing to our conservation and community development efforts. Opportunities include environmental education, tree planting, trail maintenance, and community outreach. Please contact us with your interests, skills, and availability, and we'll discuss how you can get involved.
