Our Safari Destinations

Explore Uganda's most spectacular wildlife destinations and natural wonders

1. Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site Southwestern Uganda

Home to nearly half of the world's endangered mountain gorillas, Bwindi offers an unforgettable gorilla trekking experience in one of Africa's most biodiverse forests.

Activities:

  • Mountain Gorilla Trekking
  • Bird Watching (350+ species)
  • Forest Nature Walks
  • Cultural Visits to Batwa Communities

2. Queen Elizabeth National Park

Tree-Climbing Lions Western Uganda

Uganda's most popular savanna park, famous for its diverse wildlife including the unique tree-climbing lions and the scenic Kazinga Channel.

Activities:

  • Game Drives
  • Kazinga Channel Boat Safari
  • Chimpanzee Trekking (Kyambura Gorge)
  • Tree-Climbing Lion Spotting

3. Murchison Falls National Park

World's Most Powerful Waterfall Northern Uganda

Experience the dramatic Murchison Falls where the Nile River explodes through a narrow 7-meter gorge, creating one of nature's most spectacular displays.

Activities:

  • Game Drives
  • Boat Trips to Base of Falls
  • Hiking to Top of Falls
  • Bird Watching

4. Kibale Forest National Park

Primate Capital Near Fort Portal

Home to the highest concentration of primates in East Africa, with 13 species including over 1,500 chimpanzees.

Activities:

  • Chimpanzee Tracking
  • Primate Watching (13 Species)
  • Bird Watching
  • Nature Walks

5. Rwenzori Mountains National Park

Mountains of the Moon Western Uganda

Africa's third highest mountain range with snow-capped peaks and glaciers, offering challenging mountaineering adventures.

Activities:

  • Mountaineering
  • Multi-day Hiking
  • Photography
  • Cultural Tours

6. Lake Mburo National Park

Closest to Kampala Central Uganda

The smallest of Uganda's savanna parks, perfect for walking safaris and home to zebras, antelopes, and over 350 bird species.

Activities:

  • Walking Safaris
  • Biking
  • Boat Rides
  • Horse Riding
