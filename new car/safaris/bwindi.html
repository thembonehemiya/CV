Bwindi Gorilla Trekking

Encounter mountain gorillas in their natural habitat at Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

Tour Overview

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in southwestern Uganda. It's home to almost half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, making it one of the most extraordinary wildlife experiences on earth.

Highlights

  • Once-in-a-lifetime encounter with mountain gorillas
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site with incredible biodiversity
  • Opportunity to see other primates and exotic birds
  • Cultural visits to Batwa pygmy communities
  • Beautiful montane rainforest scenery

Activities

Gorilla Tracking

Spend up to one hour with a gorilla family after finding them in the forest.

Nature Walks

Explore the forest trails with an experienced guide.

Bird Watching

Bwindi has over 350 bird species, including 23 endemic to the region.

Cultural Visits

Meet the Batwa pygmies and learn about their traditional forest lifestyle.

Getting There

Bwindi is located in southwestern Uganda, about 8-10 hours drive from Kampala or a 1-hour flight from Entebbe to Kisoro or Kihihi airstrips followed by a 1-2 hour drive.

We can arrange all transportation as part of your package, including domestic flights if preferred.

Sample Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Bwindi

After breakfast, depart from Kampala or your previous safari destination for Bwindi Impenetrable National Park. The journey takes you through beautiful landscapes with scenic views of the countryside, plantations, and the Virunga volcanoes.

Arrive in the afternoon, check in to your lodge and relax. In the evening, you'll receive a briefing from your guide about the next day's gorilla trekking experience.

Overnight: Buhoma Lodge (Mid-range) or Clouds Mountain Gorilla Lodge (Luxury)

Day 2: Gorilla Trekking

Wake up early for breakfast and transfer to the park headquarters for registration and briefing by the park rangers. You'll be assigned to a gorilla family group based on your fitness level.

The trek can take anywhere from 30 minutes to several hours through steep and sometimes muddy terrain. Once the gorillas are located, you'll spend one magical hour observing them as they go about their daily life - feeding, playing, resting, and caring for their young.

Return to the lodge for lunch and relax in the afternoon. Optional activities include a community walk to visit the Batwa pygmies or a guided nature walk in the forest.

Overnight: Buhoma Lodge (Mid-range) or Clouds Mountain Gorilla Lodge (Luxury)

Day 3: Departure

After breakfast, depart for your next destination (typically Queen Elizabeth National Park, Lake Mburo, or back to Kampala/Entebbe).

Optional extension: Add a second gorilla trekking permit for another chance to see these magnificent creatures, or visit Lake Bunyonyi for relaxation after your trek.
