June 5, 2023 By John Muhindo

Community Tree Planting Day Success

Last weekend, over 50 community members from Kyatsi II village joined our tree planting initiative along the riverbanks. This collaborative effort is part of our ongoing commitment to restore and protect the unique ecosystems of the Rwenzori Mountains.

The day began with a brief training session on proper planting techniques and the importance of native species for riverbank stabilization. Participants, including many young people and women, then worked together to plant over 200 indigenous tree seedlings from our nursery.

"This is not just about planting trees," explained Sarah Biira, our Programs Manager. "It's about building community ownership of conservation efforts and ensuring that future generations can benefit from the ecosystem services these trees provide."

The event concluded with a community lunch and discussion about future conservation activities. Many participants expressed interest in establishing their own small tree nurseries at home, which RMCI will support through training and seedling distribution.

We extend our sincere thanks to all who participated and to our local partners who helped make this event possible. Together, we are making a tangible difference in protecting the Rwenzori ecosystem.
