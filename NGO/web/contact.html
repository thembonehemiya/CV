Frequently Asked Questions

You can volunteer with RMCI by filling out the volunteer application form on our "Get Involved" page. We welcome both local and international volunteers with various skills and backgrounds. Depending on your interests and expertise, you can help with tree planting, beekeeping, skills training, or administrative support.

Your donation directly supports our conservation and community development programs. Funds are used for tree nursery operations, beekeeping equipment, vocational training materials, and community outreach activities. We ensure that donations are used efficiently and transparently, with regular updates provided to our supporters.

Yes, we welcome visitors who are interested in learning more about our work. Please contact us in advance to arrange a visit to our tree nursery, community apiary, or other project sites. We can organize guided tours that provide insights into our conservation efforts and community initiatives in the Rwenzori region.

We are open to partnerships with organizations that share our values and goals. Partnerships can take various forms, including funding support, technical collaboration, joint project implementation, or knowledge exchange. Please fill out the partnership inquiry form on our "Get Involved" page or contact us directly to discuss potential collaboration opportunities.

We primarily work in Kyatsi II village and surrounding communities in Kitholhu County, Kasese District, Western Uganda. Our focus is on the foothills and buffer zones of the Rwenzori Mountains, where we implement conservation and community development initiatives to protect the unique ecosystem while supporting sustainable livelihoods.
