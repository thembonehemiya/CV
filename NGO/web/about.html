About Us

Learn about our mission, vision, and the team behind RMCI

Our Mission

To conserve and restore the unique ecosystems of the Rwenzori Mountains while empowering local communities—especially women and youth—through sustainable land use, nature-based livelihoods, and self-employment skills training.

Our Approach

We believe that conservation and community development must go hand in hand. By creating sustainable livelihoods that depend on healthy ecosystems, we ensure long-term protection of the Rwenzori region.

Our Vision

A thriving, biodiverse, and climate-resilient Rwenzori region where nature and communities—especially women and youth—flourish together.

Our Values

  • Environmental stewardship
  • Community empowerment
  • Gender equality
  • Sustainable development
  • Transparency and accountability

Our Story

2016

RMCI was founded in Kyatsi II village, Kitholhu County, with a mission to address deforestation and support local livelihoods in the Rwenzori region.

2018

Launched our first community tree nursery, focusing on native species for riverbank restoration and erosion control.

2020

Established the community apiary program, training local farmers in beekeeping techniques and sustainable honey production.

2022

Expanded our focus to include vocational training for young mothers, recognizing the need for economic empowerment alongside conservation efforts.

2023

Distributed over 1,000 coffee seedlings to local farmers in partnership with the government, boosting sustainable livelihoods and reforestation efforts.

Our Achievements

Established Nursery

Created a thriving nursery for bamboo, cocoa, and native riverine trees, providing seedlings for reforestation efforts throughout the region.

Community Apiary

Launched a successful community apiary, enhancing biodiversity through pollination while providing sustainable income for local families.

Riverbank Restoration

Restored critical riverbank ecosystems with native vegetation, reducing erosion and improving water quality in the Rwenzori region.

Vocational Training

Initiated planning for vocational training programs to support young mothers' self-employment and economic independence.

Coffee Distribution

Distributed over 1,000 coffee seedlings to local farmers in partnership with the government—boosting sustainable livelihoods and reforestation efforts.

Community Partnerships

Established strong partnerships with local government, schools, and community groups to expand our conservation impact.
